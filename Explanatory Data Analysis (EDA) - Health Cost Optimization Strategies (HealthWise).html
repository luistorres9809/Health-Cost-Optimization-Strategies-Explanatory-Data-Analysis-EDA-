Explanatory Data Analysis (EDA) - Health Cost Optimization Strategies (HealthWise)¶

  • Name: Luis Esteban Torres Perdomo

1. Bussiness Context¶

HealthWise, our client, is a company that offers personalized and efficient medical care in the United States, with several private clinics throughout the country. Currently, HealthWise faces a serious financial situation due to a lack of efficient cost management and little awareness regarding the quality of its services. Lack of appropriate knowledge of operating cost and low control of efficiency in medical services has led to inefficient utilization of the budget with increasing financial loss and lack of long-term viability.

HealthWise does not have an evidence-based strategy in terms of how it can utilize its resources to the fullest, avoid wasteful spending and improve the quality of care it provides to its patients.

2. Objetive¶

Identify significant trends in medical spending and hospital resource utilization so that HealthWise can make sound strategic decisions.

3. Business questions and analysis approach¶

  1. How does the total cost of medical care vary by age group, and what strategic implications can these differences have for HealthWise?
  2. Which treatments or medical conditions, in the age group scoring highest in question 1, are costing the most? And how can HealthWise best manage these high-cost pediatric cases?
  3. What are the diseases that generate the highest hospital demand in HealthWise, what is their specific impact on costs?
  4. What are the variations in medical costs according to the hospital providing the service and how could HealthWise use this data to refine its business operations?
  5. Which hospitals have the highest patient volume and how do their average costs vary by medical condition?
  6. Are there significant differences in medical costs between men and women when treating the same medical conditions?

4.Theoretical framework¶

Management of hospital expenses effectively is required to achieve the economic sustainability of healthcare organizations. Various researches have revealed that hospital stay, type of disease to be treated and specific medical interventions may significantly influence operating costs (Kaplan and Porter, 2011; Porter and Teisberg, 2006). Therefore, conducting a detailed exploratory analysis makes it easy to identify self-evident areas where efficiency can be increased, unnecessary expenses reduced and usage of medical and administrative resources optimized.

The above information will help us answer question 1:

Given the number of patients and average treatment costs, the group that has the highest costs within Healhwise is the 66+ age group. This would mean that age is an important cost driver. HealthWise would do well to focus preventive interventions on older patients (especially those over 66), who are more expensive than younger ones. This finding suggests that HealthWise can more effectively target cost containment strategies and preventive interventions especially to older patients (especially those over 66), who are more expensive than younger patients.

9. Conclusion¶

This exploratory analysis allowed HealthWise to gain an overall impression of its medical cost behavior, identifying major trends in variables such as age, gender, medical condition and length of hospital stay. It emerged that the 66+ age group is the most expensive, while chronic diseases such as hypertension, diabetes and arthritis top the list in terms of cost. It was also found that asthma, although not one of the more costly diseases, generates prolonged hospital stays, another operational strain. The costs of procedures for the same disease are fluctuating in different hospitals, so HealthWise should audit why this is happening or create a standardization strategy. The breakdown by gender, however, did not show significant differences in costs.

Recommended next steps:

  • Implement predictive models to anticipate costs by condition and demographic group.
  • Design clinical and operational intervention pilots in hospitals with higher cost variability.
  • Strengthen prevention campaigns in the 66+ age group and in chronic recurrent diseases.
  • Establish mechanisms to systematically monitor gender equity in health care.

This analysis is the beginning of informed, fact-driven cost management. Its continuation and periodic revision will be critical to improving HealthWise's fiscal sustainability and quality of care.
